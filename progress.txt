MAJOR ARCHITECTURAL PIVOT

From "Tremor Spoon" to "NeuroGrip Active Stabilizer"
We realized that stabilizing a single utensil wasn't enough. We pivoted our entire mechanical architecture mid-hackathon to build a wearable, 5-finger exoskeleton glove. This allows us to provide active tremor stabilization for the entire hand, enabling users to not just eat, but write, drink, and perform daily tasks.

Current Status: SYSTEM FULLY OPERATIONAL

Hardware: 16MHz Arduino Uno driving 7 independent I2C devices and 5 analog servos simultaneously.
Power Architecture: Segregated logic and muscle power. Successfully integrated an LM2596 Buck Converter (5.0V step-down) to isolate high-current servo loads from the microcontroller.
Firmware: Custom C++ edge-computing engine with zero-latency PD control loop.


Key Engineering Challenges Conquered

During the integration phase, we encountered and solved several complex hardware and silicon-level bugs:
The Inrush Current Brownout:
Bug: Booting 5 SG90 servos simultaneously pulled ~4 Amps, collapsing the 5V line and continuously rebooting the Arduino.
Solution: Engineered a "Silent Staggered Boot" sequence. Servos are initialized without positional commands, then awakened one by one with a 200ms delay to allow the Buck Converter to stabilize the voltage spike.
Silicon-Level Sensor Freezes (I2C Lockups):
Bug: The MPU6050 IMU was sending corrupted nan data or freezing entirely on boot due to I2C bus collisions with the PCA9685 and VL53L0X array.
Solution: Wrote a Hardware Silicon Reset (0x80 command) directly to the MPU's memory registers on startup, followed by a software "Wake-Up Ping" that blocks the main loop until valid gravity vectors are confirmed.
16-Bit Integer Overflow:
Bug: Arduino's 16-bit processor crashed when calculating the magnitude of raw gravity vectors (16384 * 16384), returning zeroed angles.
Solution: Implemented explicit Float Casting prior to IMU trigonometry, preserving the math and restoring the closed-loop feedback.
SG90 Plastic Gear Protection:
Bug: Micro-vibrations from the PID loop threatened to strip the plastic gears of the SG90 servos.
Solution: Developed a Software Deadband Filter (< 1.5 degrees) and tuned the Proportional-Derivative (PD) multipliers to act as shock absorbers, protecting the physical actuators while remaining highly responsive to actual tremors.
I2C Polling Optimization (Round-Robin):
Bug: Polling 5 VL53L0X ToF lasers in a single loop created a 150ms delay, destroying the real-time tremor cancellation.
Solution: Implemented an asynchronous Round-Robin Array. The IMU and servos update at 50Hz+, while the system reads only one laser per loop, maintaining lightning-fast active stabilization alongside multi-finger therapy tracking.
